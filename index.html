It is the year 1772 in colonial Virginia. You are Betty, an enslaved black woman at Mount Vernon: the estate of George Washington. You work there as a seamstress. Previously, you were owned by Daniel and Martha Parke Custis: one of the wealthiest and most prominent families in Virginia at the time. Daniel died in 1757 and Martha received one-third of his estate, the rest going to their kids. You, Betty, are part of this one-third. Martha soon finds a husband: George Washington, who at the time was a member of the Virginia House of Burgesses and was a place where martha could relieve herself of the duties that came with the ownership of so much property.

Once you begin working at Mount Vernon, you meet a man named Andrew Judge, a white indentured tailor from England. You fall in love, but you are also interested in your childhood sweetheart Finny, who you were enslaved with at the Custis estate. You want to start a family with one of them and have a child but are torn between the two people you love.

Following George Washington's victory over the British, securing American independence, Washington gets elected as president, and you get chosen to accompany him to New York.

The country's capital later moves to Philadelphia, and you and the other slaves transfer there with the president. In Philadelphia, slavery is dying out, so there is a law granting enslaved people freedom within six months of living there, but Washington does not want to lose you and the other slaves that accompanied him to office. Washington finds a loophole: if he sends away slaves and brings them back, their six-month timer will reset, so he takes advantage of this and creates a system to keep his slaves. On one of your trips away from Philadelphia, you drown while navigating a river near Havre De Grace, Maryland.
